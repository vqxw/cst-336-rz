Instead of trying to repair damaged spinal cord pathways, researchers wirelessly
connected brain reading technology and stimulators on the spine, which they're calling
a neural bypass, in order for thoughts to be able to move limbs.

Researchers surgically implanted wireless devices into the brains of monkeys to
record brain activity.

The implants act as a bridge between the brain and spine. "They control leg motion
and stimulate muscles in real time", according to Tomislav Milekovic, a researcher at
SEPFL

This artificial interference starts off with an array of about 100 electrodes that
are implanted in the motor cortex of the brain (first implant/sensor). A computer
receives the brain signals and translates the instructions to a different array of
electrodes which are implanted near the spinal injury(second implant/sensor).
Once the second implant receives the instructions, it initiates that correct muscle
groups in the legs.
