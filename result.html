What was the result? After six days, a monkey was able to walk on a treadmill
without physiotraining.

The two implants are already in use in humans for other reasons, however, the
brain decoding software hasn't been used with humans.

Although this was a big step in helping restore movement to limbs, the
possibility is still hypothetical.

One reason it's not a fast fix for paralysis is that Humans have different
obstacles than a monkey, such as balancing on two legs vs four.

Christian Ethier, a neuroscientist at Quebec’s Université Laval, pointed out an
interesting possibility: the implants may help the body heal itself.

“By re-synchronizing the activity in the brain and spinal motor centers, they could
promote what is called ‘activity-dependent neuroplasticity,’ and consolidate any
spared connections linking the brain to the muscles", he says.

In other words, it could help the body unify brain and spinal cord messages that
that weren't getting across to each other, and aid in better recoveries versus
physical/rehabilitaiton therapy.
